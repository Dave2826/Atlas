# Atlas Roadmap

## Purpose

This roadmap defines the planned evolution of Atlas.

Development will focus on solving real operational problems before introducing advanced features.

Each phase must provide measurable value to daily store operations.

---

# Phase 1 - Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

Goal:

Replace notebooks, spreadsheets, and manual tracking with a functional retail management system.

Modules:

* Categories
* Products
* Inventory
* Customers
* Layaways
* Layaway Payments
* Vouchers
* Special Orders
* Sales
* Cash Sessions
* Expenses
* Users and Roles

Expected Outcome:

The store should be able to operate daily using Atlas as its primary management tool.

Success Criteria:

* Inventory is controlled digitally.
* Layaways are tracked digitally.
* Vouchers are tracked digitally.
* Customer information is searchable.
* Daily operations no longer depend on notebooks.

---

# Phase 2 - Operational Improvements

Goal:

Reduce manual work and improve daily efficiency.

Planned Features:

* Advanced search.
* Product filters.
* Customer history.
* Layaway summaries.
* Voucher summaries.
* Printable reports.
* Inventory alerts.
* Low-stock notifications.

Expected Outcome:

Faster customer service and improved operational visibility.

---

# Phase 3 - Communication Features

Goal:

Improve customer communication.

Planned Features:

* WhatsApp integration.
* Layaway reminders.
* Special order notifications.
* Voucher notifications.
* Payment reminders.

Expected Outcome:

Reduced forgotten layaways and improved customer engagement.

---

# Phase 4 - Business Intelligence

Goal:

Provide better decision-making information.

Planned Features:

* Sales dashboards.
* Expense dashboards.
* Inventory movement reports.
* Customer activity reports.
* Product performance analysis.
* Profitability reports.

Expected Outcome:

Better business decisions based on data.

---

# Phase 5 - Multi-Store Support

Goal:

Support businesses operating from multiple locations.

Planned Features:

* Store management.
* Shared inventory.
* Inventory transfers.
* Multi-location reporting.
* Centralized administration.

Expected Outcome:

Atlas becomes suitable for growing retail businesses.

---

# Phase 6 - Commercial Product

Goal:

Prepare Atlas for use by other businesses.

Planned Features:

* Installation wizard.
* Business configuration tools.
* Branding customization.
* Backup management.
* User onboarding tools.

Expected Outcome:

Atlas becomes deployable to multiple retail businesses.

---

# Future Exploration

The following ideas may be evaluated in future versions:

* Artificial intelligence assistant.
* Purchase recommendations.
* Demand forecasting.
* Automated inventory suggestions.
* Mobile application.
* Cloud synchronization.
* Barcode support.
* Label printing support.

These features are intentionally postponed until the operational foundation is fully validated.

---

# Development Philosophy

The success of Atlas will not be measured by the number of features implemented.

Success will be measured by how effectively Atlas solves real operational problems and improves daily business workflows.
